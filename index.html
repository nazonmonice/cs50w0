What's so cool about scuba diving?

Scuba diving gives you the opportunity to interact with your favorite marine animals and even discover some new ones.You enter a totally new world that is unthinkable, unimaginable and is ever changing. Scuba diving is like being a child again with sunken treasures, colourful creatures and mysterious new worlds all presenting an incredible insight into what it feels like to be almost totally naïve and wondrous about the environment in which you find yourself.

The waters of The Bahamas are spectacular from space but under the waves is where the beauty awaits. With The Bahamas being made up of 700 islands and cays, there are so many wrecks, blue holes, reefs and more that have be to explored! With their warm water, vibrant sun and breathtakinly beautiful blue seas, The Bahamas makes a great destination for scuba diving.

Here are some fun facts about The Bahamas:

  • The Bahamas has the third largest barrier reef on the planent: Stretching more than 190 miles in length, the Andros Barrier Reef is one of the biggest barrier reefs in the world – and the third largest living organism on the planet! It sits along the edge of an underwater trench called the Tongue of the Ocean, running parallel to the east coast of the island of Andros.
  • Hollywood loves Paradise: The Bahamas is a popular destination for filming movies in fact, more than 30 films have been made in The Bahamas i.e. Pirates of the Caribbean, Thunderball, Casino Royale and Into the Deep.
  • The Bahamas has one of the longest known underwater cave systems in the world: The underwater cave is 6 miles long and can be found at the Lucayan National Park, Grand Bahama.
  • The origin of the name "The Bahamas" is Spanish: The name “Bahamas” comes from the Spanish words “baja mar”, meaning shallow water or sea.
